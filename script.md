# CF Industries Pitch Script

2 minutes. Left: slides. Right: dashboard.

---

## [SLIDE 1 — Title]

"CF Industries. Ticker CF. We're going long at 130 dollars, targeting 185. That's 42% upside with a 2.5 to 1 risk-reward.

Here's the setup."

## [SLIDE 2 — The Crisis]

"We're on Day 32 of Operation Epic Fury. The US military has effectively shut down the Strait of Hormuz — where 20% of the world's oil flows through every day.

Ship transits collapsed from 138 a day to 2. Almost 2,000 vessels stranded. Oil jumped from 75 to over 104 dollars a barrel."

## [SLIDE 3 — Why CF Wins]

"So why a fertilizer company?

CF Industries uses American natural gas to make fertilizer. They pay $2.89. Their overseas competitors? $15 or more — five times higher. That's the widest cost gap in a decade.

And it gets better. Qatar — one of the biggest fertilizer exporters — declared force majeure. They literally can't ship product. CF fills that gap with domestic production."

## [SLIDE 4 — Where Wall Street Is Wrong] + [DASHBOARD: Tab 3 — Why Wall Street Is Wrong]

"Now here's where it gets interesting. Wall Street thinks this resolves by April. Their models have CF earning about 31 dollars per share next year.

But look at prediction markets — real people betting real money.

*(gesture to dashboard)*

68% chance US forces enter Iran by April 30. Only 25% chance Hormuz reopens by mid-April. 52% chance oil hits $120.

If the crisis lasts through planting season, CF could earn 38 to 50 per share. That's way above consensus."

## [SLIDE 5 — Fundamentals]

"The company is solid. 7.1 billion in revenue, 46.5% EBITDA margin — best in the industry by far. Nutrien does 22%. Mosaic, 18%.

Analysts rate it HOLD with a $115 target — they haven't caught up."

## [SLIDE 6 — Four Possible Futures] + [DASHBOARD: Tab 1 — Investment Simulator]

"We modeled four scenarios. Even the base case — 45% probability — gives us 25% return.

*(switch to dashboard simulator)*

To make it real: invest the price of an iPhone — $1,199. Expected value after one year? $1,559. That's 360 bucks. 51 boba teas.

Worst case with our stop loss? We lose $204. Best case? We nearly double it."

## [SLIDE 7 — Risk Management]

"Risk is clear. Stop loss at $108 caps our downside at 17%. We walk away if Hormuz reopens quickly, gas spikes, or demand collapses.

The asymmetry is what matters. We risk 17% to make 42%."

## [SLIDE 8 — Catalyst Calendar]

"The catalysts are stacked. Trump's Iran deadline April 6. Sanctions waiver expires April 19. CF reports Q1 earnings in May — the first quarter with Hormuz premium baked in.

Everything falls within our timeframe."

## [SLIDE 9 — The Trade] + [DASHBOARD: Tab 5 — The Trade]

"So — long CF at 130.53. Target 185. Stop 108. Expected return: plus 30%, probability-weighted. Conviction is high.

This is a second-order trade that consensus hasn't priced. The cost advantage is real, the supply disruption is confirmed, and the catalysts are weeks away.

Thank you."
